STAR Reflection

S Situation

In a contested support modification case in Henry County, I was called to testify regarding income calculations for a self-employed individual who had been underreporting earnings across three years.

T Task

My task was to explain the methodology by which we compiled and verified Opposing Party's actual income across several sources clearly and concisely, and to defend that methodology on cross-examination.

A Action

I prepared detailed spreadsheets breaking down income from bank deposits, Venmo, Apple Cash, and an appointment book. I meticulously cross-referenced all sources to avoid double-counting, and presented my analysis to the Court.

R Result

My attorney won the case. The unreported income I attributed to the opposing party was imputed to her in the Court's Order alongside a finding of fact that directly referenced my testimony.

02

Problem Solver

NACE Critical Thinking Competency · ABA Guideline 4

Competency Development

Analytical thinking and strategic decision-making under pressure.

The legal profession does not wait for convenient moments to present its challenges. Managing countless active cases simultaneously requires constant prioritization, creative problem solving, and an ever-present awareness of deadlines.


Throughout my professional career, I have risen to these challenges. I have drafted complex pleadings, reviewed hundreds of pages of documents to create summary exhibits, and developed a custom solution to track incoming and outgoing Discovery.


This reflects the ABA's Guideline 4 emphasis on analytical competence and the NACE "Critical Thinking/Problem Solving" competency — the ability to exercise sound judgment, identify resources, and solve novel problems in a professional context.

STAR Reflection

S Situation

Our office lacked an efficient way to keep track of Discovery, both incoming and outgoing. This naturally created several issues (such as not being aware Discovery Requests had been sent until we received a 26(F) letter, not knowing due dates, and not knowing if our responses had been sent.)

T Task

I tasked myself to find an effective solution that would address all of these issues in one platform.

A Action

I researched programs and eventually decided to use Asana to create a custom system that allowed multiple users to calendar and prioritize incoming and outgoing discovery in real time, automatically calendared deadlines, sent reminders, and kept all of the information in one place.

R Result

The system was and is incredibly effective. I am proud to say that nothing has fallen through the cracks, and I was able to keep the Attorneys apprised of Discovery at every step. What would have taken twenty minutes to verify instead took twenty seconds.
